Bicycles & Cities

The bicycle, though older than more widely used transportation means, as the bike and ‎the car, is generally less common and preferable among urban residents of cities.

‎ There are some exceptions though, that show us that there is still space for this eco-‎friendly vehicle. We can, for example, look at the cities of Volos and Trikala in Thessaly, ‎ Greece, were the vast majority of people own a bicycle;‎

they tend to use it, instead of their car, for their everyday movements inside the city. ‎

‎ There, the bicycle prevailed other means of transport, because of some special urban ‎features: The flatness of the urban landscape, the small size that keeps distances not so ‎big, and the street plan that has as a result only square blocks of buildings, square ‎junctions and safer crossings for the bicycles to pass through.‎
